Call Element: Opening and Introduction


Call Opening & Greeting: The Call Opening & Greeting is the first opportunity to interact with a customer. The greeting should be concise yet friendly.

  • The VSSC provides the customer with their first name and the approved script.


Script (Outbound): Prior to the Purpose of Call and the Probing & Consulting elements the VSSC will communicate to the Customer, that the call is being recorded.


“I’m calling from the Software Solutions Consulting Group for FedEx, may I speak to “Customer Name.” “Customer Name” before we begin, I want to let you know that this call may be recorded for Quality purposes.”

If the Customer expresses concern with the call being recorded, does not consent to the call being recorded, or expresses dissatisfaction with the information provided, the VSSC will state the following and end the call:


“I will notify your Account Executive with an update of your request. In addition, to provide the service you need, a FedEx Representative will contact you shortly for further assistance.”


  • If someone answers other than the Customer Contact noted on the PID, the VSSC will ask to speak to the Customer Contact using the following greeting:


“I’m calling from the Software Solutions Consulting Group for FedEx, may I speak to “Customer Name.” Agent will repeat the Call Opening and Greeting to the Customer Contact.


If the customer is not available and a voice mail system is reached the VSSC will leave a message for the customer with the pre-determined voice mail message scripting.







  • Purpose of Call:

  • The ‘Purpose of Call’ must be completed prior to commencing probing or Customer training.

  • The VSSC informs the customer why they are calling. Provides an overview of the goals of the call per original training request and training needs determined during previous contact call(s) made to the Customer.

  • Prior to starting the training, the VSSC assures the customer that they will be thoroughly trained on the FedEx solution(s) that best suit their business needs, and that all of their questions will be addressed. The approach should reassure the customer from a general standpoint, as well as to any specific concerns.

  • Agent must not provide the customer with an opening to postpone training.


Examples:

    • “I'm calling you today in reference to the _________ (application) training your Account Executive requested for you.”

  • "Before we finish the call you should know how to.....

  • “If there is something that I’m unable to help you with, I’ll be more than happy to connect you with someone in another FedEx Department who can help you.”

  • “Feel free to ask specific questions throughout our call.”










Call Element: Technical Knowledge & Training


  • Probing & Consulting:

  • The VSSC probes for information that will enable them to meet the customer’s training needs, starting by reviewing all previous information gathered during consult calls.

  • Probing questions should be tailored to the specific solution and the customer’s business needs.

    • Refer to Training Checklists and all applicable articles in the Knowledgebase for a listing of subjects.

  • The VSSC probes to determine the customer’s role in the company as well as identify the type of items the customer ships outbound.

  • The VSSC also identifies, by active listening, additional FedEx products or features that meet the customer’s needs and then makes appropriate recommendations throughout the call.



Example for FedEx.com:

Shipping – Domestic and/or International, Multi-Piece Shipments, Group Shipments, etc.

Tracking – Date Range Needed, Exceptions, etc.

Address Book – New Addresses, Group Shipping, etc.

Preferences – Defaults, Processing Return Labels, Shipping Profile

Fast Ship – New Profile

Reports – Business Need Specifics














  • Training Procedure:

  • Registration of the customer for a solution is not considered Training.

  • VSSC effectively trains the customer to all applicable elements for the correctly determined desired solution.

  • Information provided is accurate.

  • VSSC verifies that each training request has been addressed before moving onto the next training topic. i.e. “Do you have any questions on what we just covered?”

  • VSSC explains the benefits of the elements of training and the steps they are recommending.

  • VSSC offers to stay on line to walk the customer through any steps

  • VSSC takes ownership of any questions or issues until resolution, transfer, or workaround is determined.

  • VSSC must always demonstrate that the customer’s needs were met through appropriate testing, asking if the customer has a package they are shipping that day.

    • If no package available, the VSSC must ask for the use of a real address from the Customer for use when preparing a “test” package for shipment.

    • If the solution to be trained does not include shipping, the agent must ask for the use of past invoice for use during training.

    • If the solution to be trained includes reporting, the agent must ask to customize or run an actual report.


Example:

At the outset of an International Training VSSC asks if the customer has an actual International package that they plan to ship that day. If so, VSSC walks the customer through preparing that shipment, and arranging an actual pick-up.










  • Utilize Available Resources:

  • The VSSC uses all available and appropriate resources to fulfill the training request and/or resolve any questions or errors encountered.

  • Resources can include Knowledge Base, the applicable Shipping Application/Solution, FedEx back-end systems, GoToAssist, Supervisors, Mentors, Senior Support, or other departments.

  • Unacceptable practices include transferring a call to the wrong department, foregoing the use of the Resources, or refusing to escalate a call when requested by the customer.









Call Element: Professionalism and Soft Skills


  • Active Listening Skills:

    • Demonstrates an interest in the customer and their business, with the intent of helping. The agent remains focused on the customer's training needs and uses the information the customer provides to complete the training and maximize its outcome.

  • The VSSC does not interrupt the customer.

  • The VSSC responds appropriately & promptly to the customer’s inquiries and comments, demonstrating that they are fully engaged with the customer.

  • Avoids requiring the customer to repeat themselves more than once.

    • Note: This does not apply when the Agent asks the customer to repeat information because it is difficult to hear or understand the customer due to background noise, poor phone connection, customer’s accent/rate of speech, etc.






  • Overall Communication Skills, Call Warmth, Reassurance and Empathy: The VSSC communicates in a clear and concise manner throughout the call.

  • VSSC makes an effort to establish rapport with the customer throughout the call, helping to customize each customer’s experience.

  • Adapts to the customer's level of expertise, yet remains professional. VSSC communicates and uses the appropriate terminology based on the customer's skill set.

  • Uses correct grammar.

  • Is aware of the volume.

  • The VSSC acknowledges emotions expressed by the customer with appropriate responses to those emotions expressed by the customer (either positive or negative). Empathetic statements should be provided immediately following the customer’s description of their problem.

  • Initiates brief non-call related conversation when appropriate.

  • The VSSC will use an upbeat tone of voice.

  • The VSSC does not sound robotic, repetitive or as if reading from a script throughout the call.







  • Call Control: The VSSC manages and leads the call efficiently without compromising customer focus or effectiveness. The Agent stays engaged with the customer and helps keep the customer on track.

Call Control includes:

  • Respecting the customer's time.

  • Guiding the pace of training to match the customer (not too slow or fast).

  • Avoiding unexplained dead air, long silences, or pauses.

  • Utilizing a logical and efficient progression from starting point to ending point throughout the call process.

  • Working to keep the call moving forward by offering assistance where needed and not waiting for the customer to drive the next step. The agent focuses ‘rambling or straying customers’ on the purpose of the call.

  • Gathering and/or providing only information that is relevant to the call, thereby avoiding unnecessarily lengthening the call.

  • Refraining from initiating or contributing to extended non-business related conversation with the customer.







  • Hold / Transfer Procedure:

Hold: The hold button must be used any time a VSSC needs to disengage from the customer by not speaking to him/her for 30 seconds or longer. (The VSSC may need to call another department or research the customer’s issue/question in the KB, etc.)

In the event that a VSSC needs to place a customer on hold, they should always:

      • Gain permission from the customer first. This should be presented in the form of a question. e.g. “Do you mind if I….?” or “May I place you on hold for (length of time)?” And allow the customer’s agreement to the question.

      • Provide a reason for placing them on hold

      • Provide an expectation for the length of hold time prior to placing them on hold

      • Upon return, thank the customer for holding

      • Refresh the customer every 3 minutes.


When a customer requests to place a VSSC on hold:

  • The VSSC is to hold for no longer than 5 minutes.

  • The VSSC may disconnect the call if the hold is longer than 5 minutes. They are then required to immediately call the customer back.

Transfer: Transfer procedures should be used any time a VSSC must transfer a customer to another FedEx department for further assistance. The VSSC should ensure that the transfer process is seamless causing little or no inconvenience for the customer.


Transfer procedure includes the following:

      • Informing the customer that he/she will be transferred to another department for further assistance, and stating the name of the department to which the call will be transferred.

      • When transferring to another department, the VSSC offers the toll-free number for that Department and the NLU verbiage (if applicable), then disconnects after the customer is introduced to the FedEx Representative in that department.

      • Providing the transfer department with the customer’s information.

      • When transferring, VSSCs must introduce the customer to the FedEx Representative receiving the transfer (must introduce the FedEx Representative by using their first name).

      • VSSCs should never cold transfer calls unless approved by FedEx.


Conference: Every time a conference with another FedEx department is needed the VSSC is required to:

  • Gain permission from the customer first. This should be presented in the form of a question e.g. “Do you mind if I….?” Or “May I place you on hold for (length of time)?” and allow the customer’s agreement to the question.

  • Provide a time frame to which they will adhere to hold

  • Provide a reason for hold including the department joining the call.

  • When the FedEx Representative from the department is on the line, introduce and provide an overview of the reason for the conference.

  • When the conference is complete, Thank the FedEx Representative and let them know they are free to leave the call.


Notes:

  • Even if the VSSC returns to the customer during a hold procedure and apologizes for the delay, the VSSC must still meet the three-minute “refresh” requirement. An apology for a delay beyond three minutes does not negate the VSSC’s “N” score if he/she did not return to the customer within three minutes.

  • If multiple hold procedures are used during the call, the VSSC must follow the proper hold procedure for each hold occurrence. Failure to use the proper hold procedure on any of the multiple holds will result in a rating of “N” for the call element.







  • Professional Demeanor: The VSSC handles the call in a professional manner, treating both the customer and FedEx with respect throughout the call. The VSSC follows all professional conduct processes and procedures, refrains from creating confrontational situations, and offers professional assistance at all times.

  • Represents FedEx in the highest professional manner.

  • Maintains a positive attitude, always placing FedEx products, processes and employees in the best light.



The VSSC will receive a “0” rating in Professional Conduct for any of the following behaviors, but not limited to:

  • Providing personal information (about themselves) to the customer (e.g., personal phone/ number, email address, home address, etc.).

  • Creating distracting noises such as (but not limited to), talking to self or peers, eating or chewing while handling customer calls.

  • Finishing a conversation with a peer as a call is coming in, when those monitoring the call can hear the comments.

  • Using a language other than the language the caller is using (e.g. English caller – the VSSC must stay in the English language).

  • The VSSC interacts with the customer in a too “casual” manner, compromising the professional customer/client relationship.

  • The VSSC advises transferring FedEx Representative to place customer on hold prior to transfer, instead of accepting call and then placing customer on hold.



The VSSC will receive a “Critical” rating in Professional Conduct for any of the following behaviors:

  • Making negative comments to an internal or external customer about FedEx services, other FedEx departments/employees, FedEx business partners/suppliers, FedEx competitors, FedEx systems, or FedEx in general.

    • Must be derogatory comment; Simply stating that the system/computer is moving slowly or is down will not receive a “Critical” rating.

  • Blaming other FedEx departments/employees for problems or issues.

  • Using rude, argumentative, or confrontational language directed at the customer, such as:

    • Using verbal insults or calling the customer a derogatory name.

    • Antagonizing or provoking the customer through accusatory personal statements.

    • Using sarcasm that insults the customer’s intelligence or motivation at any time during the call.

  • Engaging in racial or sexually oriented conversation (including telling “off-color” jokes).

  • Hanging up on a customer, whether intentional or not.

  • Ignoring the incoming call by:

    • Refusing to acknowledge or speak to the customer (by not giving a “live” greeting or not speaking following the automated greeting) when those monitoring the call can hear the customer on the line.

  • Immediately placing the customer on hold at the beginning of the call without asking or informing the customer of the hold.

  • Making negative comments about any customer on an open customer line (current customer, previous customer, or customers in general), including while a customer has the Agent on hold. This also includes making negative comments about a previous customer to the current customer.

  • Using profanity at any time on an open customer line.

  • Dropping in or transferring calls to departments that are closed at the time of transfer.

  • Refusing to honor any customer request for escalation after first attempting to resolve the issue.

  • Refusing to provide the customer with the VSSC’s name or employee number or giving a false name/number, when the customer asks for this information.


Notes:

The critical professionalism behavior must be blatant and obvious.








Call Element: Closing


  • Recap / “Next Step”:

  • The VSSC reviews ‘goals of the call’ per the original PID request, Purpose of Call, and the initial Consulting & Probing and confirms they these goals were achieved.

  • The VSSC confirms customer is comfortable with outcome and assists if they have additional questions.

  • The VSSC provides reassurance that they are delighted to help with additional questions, and that assistance is always available if they have questions later

  • VSSC offers all applicable Support phone numbers.









  • Call Closing


  • The VSSC offers a call close that is appropriate and brands FedEx.

  • The closing pace should be appropriate, not rushed or too slow.

  • VSSC has a positive tone.

  • If transferring the customer to another department at this time, VSSC uses the transfer procedures as outlined in the above mentioned ‘Hold/Transfer Procedure’ section.

Notes:

A “Y” will be scored for Call Close if the call is completed and the customer hangs up before the Agent has a chance to say or complete a call close.








Call Element: Documentation


  • Journalization in Nexus and Webcalendar:

  • All documentation in Nexus and Webcalendar is accurate, concise, and complete demonstrating that the PID was handled according to process.

    • Refer to the Knowledgebase for a listing of requirements.

  • If a call did does meet the expected time allotted, the documentation includes a justification as to why this occurred.

  • Indication of Implementation is accurate.

  • Resources used by VSSC to complete the training must be cited.

    • If KB is used, the article number must be included in Nexus comments.

    • If No Resources are used, this must be noted as well.

  • In addition, the Webcalendar entry is accurately logged with correct training time and PID related information.


Examples of how to site the utilized resources:

KB #12276

Lead used

GTA used

FedEx.com used

Called FedEx.com Tech Support – Case# 12345678

No resources used









  • Nexus Time Log Entry:

  • Reflects accuracy in logging of Begin Time / End Time within a maximum of a 10 minute "grace period".

  • Any discrepancies of the time taken for the call are justified in the documentation. This will be scored in "Journalization in Nexus and Webcalendar".







Bonus Element:


  • Value Add

  • VSSC suggests additional FedEx solutions that would benefit the Customer.

  • The agent will probe for this need throughout the call.

  • Training shall be provided for the value added solution during the current training session.

  • When time does not permit, a future appointment will be scheduled with the customer.








  • Critical Elements: If the VSSC conducts any Critical behaviors, the corresponding element in the call is to be scored as “0”.

  • Business Critical: Probing

    • VSSC fails to probe to obtain relevant information regarding the customer’s use of the solution in their business. Agent fails to tailor their questions based on customer information to best determine the correct training approach.

  • Business Critical: Professionalism

    • VSSC makes negative comments to an internal or external customer about FedEx services, other FedEx departments/employees, FedEx business partners/suppliers, FedEx competitors, FedEx systems, or FedEx in general.

    • Misrepresented themselves beyond their support boundaries.

    • Intentionally falsified information provided to the customer. I.e. name, PID, etc.

  • Business Critical: Journalization in Nexus and Web calendar

    • VSSC does not thoroughly document Nexus as required.

  • End User Critical: Training Procedure

    • VSSC does not effectively train the customer to all applicable elements for the correct determined desired solution.

  • End User Critical: Training Procedure

    • VSSC provides inaccurate information that may cause unnecessary expense to the customer.

  • End User Critical: Professionalism

    • VSSC exhibits behaviors such as sarcasm, racially or sexually oriented conversation, verbally insulting the customer, using profanity, refusing to take a call, refusing to escalate to a supervisor, hanging up on the customer, etc.
