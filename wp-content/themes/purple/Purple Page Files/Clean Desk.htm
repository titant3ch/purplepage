Overview:

 

Our clients entrust us with information to enable us to provide valuable marketing services for them and on their behalf.  Much of this information is proprietary and confidential, and some of this information is personal, even sensitive in nature.  Harte-Hanks places tremendous importance on carefully handling information from our clients, their customers and about our business and people.  In order to do this effectively, we need everyone's help.  Because of the nature of some of the information we handle, it is essential that we have what is called a "clean desk" policy to help safeguard this proprietary, sensitive, and personal information. 

 

In adhering to this policy, you must assure that no confidential business information and no personal information of our clients, their customers or other team members in any form (printed form, on your computer or in writing) is left unsupervised at your workspace.  When leaving your workspace, it is your responsibility to secure all confidential business and sensitive personal information according to our company policies and procedures.  Please reference the Confidential Communications Policy set forth in Section 2.10 of the Employee Handbook as well as your confidentiality agreement with Harte-Hanks.

 

You must also secure your desk at the end of the work day.  Do not leave valuables and/or other personal articles in or around your workstation which might attract unauthorized individuals to your work space

 

In addition to the safeguarding of proprietary and confidential information elements, the Clean Desk Policy also includes expectations of:  1) maintaining a professional, efficient, clean and safe working environment, and 2) protecting Harte-Hanks equipment and property. 

 

The following provides a summary of the key expectations around the Clean Desk Policy:

 

•       Hard copy reference material and cube notes should be organized and limited. Online portals should be used as much as possible.

•       No pens/pencils should be used at the agent desks - - this includes non-SPI accounts. 

•       No badges left unattended.

•       No one, including Supervisors, should have cell phones or other electronic equipment (Kindle, iPads, games, cameras, MP3 players, ear buds, etc.) on their desktops or visible. 

•       Cell phones and other wireless devices may not be used at your work station.  If you need to use your cell phone, you may do so during your break periods outside the building or in the break room. 

•       Cell phone or other equipment chargers are not permitted.  No USB flash drives permitted.

•       Taking photos with your cell phone camera (or any other camera) may never occur within the building.

•       All SPI Accounts will be required to use lockers for personal items (i.e., no bags/backpacks/ purses/etc., at the desk).  You must provide your own lock.

•       If the account is not required to use lockers, bags/backpacks/purses/etc., should be kept on the floor deep underneath your desk.  You are encouraged to use a locker for these items.

•       Jackets, sweaters, blanket throws, etc. should be stored on the backs of chairs or in lockers, not on desk tops.

•       No computers are to be left unlocked (i.e., remind your staff to "ctrl+alt+delete" before they walk away from their desk).

•       Desks should be kept free of clutter and reading material (if account policies allow reading between calls).  The amount of material at each desk should be limited and should be cleared away at the end of each shift.

•       Personal items and decorations should be kept to a minimum.

•       Breaks should be taken away from the desk.

•       No meals on the call center floor are allowed (exception for account-level events).

•       Snacks and drinks should be kept to a minimum.  Only dry (vending-type) snacks are allowed at your desk.

•       Only bottles or cups with solid lids are allowed on the call center floor.  Soft drink cans or fast food drinks with flimsy plastic lids are not allowed. 

 

Daily walk-throughs will be conducted - - each account will be assessed a Pass or Fail rating - - any single violation will cause the account to fail.  Updated components of the Clean Desk Walk-through include periodic spot checks of paperwork on desktops and asking people to pick things up off their desktop (ex: newspapers) so that we can see what is actually on the desktop.

 

Additionally, please keep in mind that we are continuing to proceed with eliminating email from the majority of all agents. We are also updating the Agent Load Profiles to streamline/limit the software that is loaded on each machine for each team.

 

 

Policy Specifics:

 

Printed Material:

If you use printed materials (reports, lists, order forms), you are responsible for the secure storage and proper disposal of that material, either in a bin marked for shredding or to shred such documents yourselves.  Printed materials subject to the “Clean Desk” policy include any materials obtained from local printers, network printers, faxes, manually distributed reports and hard-copy materials received from external sources.  Do not leave printed materials on copier/printers or on nearby tables.

 

Pens/Pencils:

Writing instruments such as pens/pencils may not be used on the majority of accounts/desks located on the call center floor (management cubicles not included). If the account/program you work on requires the use of pens/pencils, your manager must provide the Director of Quality & Training with a business justification statement along with details regarding the exemption. If you are approved to use pens/pencils to complete work tasks, or if you are located in one of the management cubicles, it is your responsibility to see that they are put away and secured when you are not at your desk. Adherence to this section will be audited by a Clean Desk walk-through. See Clean Desk Walk-through Process. 

 

Internet Access and E-mail:

You may have been provided Internet access solely for work purposes.  You should familiarize yourself with the Internet and Electronic Mail Policy in the Employee Handbook (Section 2.8) regarding your use of the Internet.  Harte-Hanks monitors Internet traffic and usage on its systems, servers and other equipment.  Any violations of policy will be addressed.  Your access to Web sites has been limited, and you should only visit Web sites required for work related purposes.

 

You have been provided access to e-mail for work purposes only.  Again, familiarize yourself with the Internet and Electronic Mail policy in the Employee Handbook (Section 2.8).

 

Your Computer:

You have been provided software specific to your job function.  You may not modify the computer’s functionality or settings, or load any other software onto the computer to which you have been provided access.  No USB flash drives are allowed.  See the External Software Policy (2.9) in the Employee Handbook with respect to your use of the computer.

 

Be aware if other people are looking over your shoulder when you are inputting or using any sensitive data.  If you believe it is appropriate for you to have a screen filter to help prevent others from viewing your screen, please speak to your manager.

 

Be sure your password protected screensaver is activated before you leave your work area.  When you leave your desk at the end of your shift, be sure to log off from your computer.  If you use a laptop at your desk, you must lock it in a secure location or take it home at the end of your workday.

 

Phones:

No one, including Supervisors, should have cell phones or other electronic equipment (Kindle, iPads, games, cameras, MP3 players, ear buds, etc.) on their desktops or visible.  You may store them in your handbag or pockets.  Cell phone or other equipment chargers are not permitted. 

 

Cell phones and other wireless devices may not be used at your work station.  If you need to use your cell phone, you may do so during your break periods outside the building or in the break room.  Taking photos with your cell phone camera (or any other camera) may never occur within the building. Adherence to this section will be audited by a Clean Desk walk-through. See Clean Desk Walk-through Process. 

 

You may not make personal telephone calls from your desk.  Be mindful that telephone calls made from your workstation may be monitored by Harte-Hanks and its clients.

 

Note: There may be Special Circumstances which require an exemption to the cell phone policy.  Such exemptions must be approved by the HR department and need to be specific. HR will keep these exemptions, in writing, on file. Additionally, HR will inform the supervisor of such exemptions who must in turn notify the Quality & Training Department.

 

Purses/Backpacks/Clothing Items:

Purses, backpacks and all other bags are not allowed to sit on the desktops of desks located on the call center floor. All SPI Accounts will be required to use lockers.  If the account is not required to use lockers, bags/backpacks/purses/etc., should be kept on the floor deep underneath your desk.  You are encouraged to use a locker for these items.  Jackets, sweaters, blanket throws, etc. should be stored on the backs of chairs or in lockers, not on desk tops.  Adherence to this section will be audited by a Clean Desk walk-through. See Clean Desk Walk-through Process.

 

Food and Drinks:

Breaks should be taken away from the desk.  No meals on the call center floor are allowed (exception for account-level events).  Snacks and drinks should be kept to a minimum.  Only dry (vending-type) snacks are allowed at your desk.  Only bottles or cups with solid lids are allowed on the call center floor.  Soft drink cans or fast food drinks with flimsy plastic lids are not allowed. 

 

 

Summary:

 

Each member of the Harte-Hanks team must act in a manner to safeguard the assets of the business.  Since our client’s customers’ data are one of our most valuable assets, it is incumbent on each and every one of us to do our part.

 

If any person fails to keep a "clean desk" in accordance with this policy, including, but not limited to, taking unauthorized notes or taking confidential data out from his or her work area without proper authorization, she or he will be subject to corrective action, up to and including termination of employment.

 

Any person who believes that anyone is violating this policy is strongly urged to report it without delay to his or her department manager or Managing Director.  Alternatively, employees may report such conduct to the manager of Human Resources at their unit, Andrew Harrison (Head of HR for HDM, 512-434-1116), or the Harte-Hanks toll-free HR Hotline (866-480-2667); voicemail messages may be left at either of these numbers 24 hours a day, seven days a week.
