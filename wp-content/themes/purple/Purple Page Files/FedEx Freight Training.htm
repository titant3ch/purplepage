Bill Of Lading Walkthrough

Account Selection

You can select the account number you wish to use from the drop down. If you select “Account no.”, you can enter an account number to use. However, you can only use Shipper or Consignee with a Shipper Account Number, and you can only use Third Party with a Bill To Account Number.

Payment Type/Terms

You can select the Payment type, which can be Shipper, Consignee, or Third Party. You can select the Payment terms, which can be Prepaid, Collect, or Collect – Section 7 signed.

From/To

When using a Shipper account number, the From or To will be populated with your address depending on whether you choose Payment type Shipper or Consignee. You can select an address from your Address Book. In addition, you can save addresses to your Address Book. This only happens when you actually process the shipment. Only the items marked with an asterix (*) are required.

Third Party Information

Only necessary when doing a Third Party or Bill To shipment.

Service Selection

FedEx Freight Priority and Economy are the normal Freight account types. The others are part of FedEx Freight Guaranteed Services, and are more expensive.

Shipment Details

Handling Units: The number of skids/pallets or whatever package type you’re using that has the same pieces, class, weight, and description. When using more than one handling unit on the same line, values of pieces, weight, and description

Packaging Type: Choose a packaging type.

Pieces: Number of pieces on the skid or skids.

Class: The NMFC class is a calculation based on density. L”xW”xH” / 1,728. That equals the cubic feet. Then divide the weight by the cubic feet. Then compare the cubic feet with the NMFC guidelines provided by the NMFTA here.

Weight: Enter the weight of all of the handling units on this line.

Description: Enter a description of the product.

Add Row: If you have multiple skids with different pieces, classes, weights, and descriptions, add a new row for each skid.

Total handling units: The total of all handling units from each row.

Pro Number: Always have FedEx assign PRO number.

Additional Services: Choose any applicable additional services. Additional fees may apply.

Submit Pickup with BOL: When doing mock shipments, NEVER schedule a pickup. For live shipments, you probably want to always schedule a pickup. BOL’s and Shipping Labels that do not have a pickup scheduled for them are just pieces of paper. There’s no need to cancel anything.

Shipment Options

Create Shipping Labels: Number of labels should always equal the total handling units.

Special Instructions: These appear on the BOL, not on the shipping label.

Custom Delivery: When the customer requests a delivery between certain times.

Collect On Delivery: Pretty standard. Just FYI “Remit To” means to whom the payment is going.

Declared Value: You can choose an amount per weight, a new value, or mark as used or reconditioned.

Email Notifications: You can have email notifications sent to the shipper, consignee, third party, and up to 3 other addresses. In addition, you can send the bill of lading and shipping labels with the emails.

International Shipment: Broker Information: Shipments to CA or MX always require broker information to be filled out.

Template Name: You can save the sender and pallet in a template to use later.

Rate this shipment: Will show a popup with a detailed rate quote.

Submit: Click to access PDF of BOL and Shipping Labels. As long as you didn’t schedule a pickup, no need to cancel an order if you generated bills of lading. They’re pieces of paper until the shipment is picked up.

Custom Reports Walkthrough

Report Type

Report Type: Select the report type you want.

 Manage Accounts

Accounts available: Add the accounts available to the Accounts added box by selecting the account and pressing “Add to report”.

Report Type

Service Type: Choose the service type. Usually “All”.

Report Type: Choose the service type. Usually “Detailed with Earned Discount”.

Shipment Type: Check Shipment type. Usually check Outbound and Inbound.

Sorty By: Choose the sort option. Usually “Pickup date”.

Delivery Type: Choose the delivery type. Usually pick “Email spreadsheet data…”. Enter up to 3 email addresses.

Frequency

One-time report: Choose a date range up to a Month.

Daily Recurring report: Enter the time in 00:00 format CST on the days you want the report emailed to you. Instead of filling in for all 7 days of the week, just put the time into “Everyday”.

Weekly Recurring report: Choose the day or days of the week that you want the report to be sent in 00:00 format CST.

Bi-weekly Recurring report: Enter a time to receive biweekly report. Report will be sent at the end of the time period. First time period is 1st-15th. Second is the 16th-end of the month.

Monthly Recurring report: Select the day of the month and the time.

Quarterly: Select the time of day.

Every Recurring Report: Choose a time and a number that represents a report every x days.

Next: Go to the next page.

Report Name: Choose a report name.

Send Request: Create the report.
